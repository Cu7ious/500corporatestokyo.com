Speakers

Below are biographies of some of the 500 Startups team members who will speak at the program. Please note that all speakers are subject to confirmation.


Marvin Liao

Partner, 500 Startups

Marvin is a Partner at 500 Startups and is in charge of the San Francisco seed program. Marvin is a former senior executive in the media and technology space in both large and start-up growth companies, including Yahoo!, and has experience managing large multifunctional teams across multiple countries. Marvin specializes in business development, sales & marketing and international market entry.


Zafer Younis

Partner, 500 Startups

Zafer Younis is a Partner at 500 Startups, the world's most active early stage tech investor. Zafer’s work at 500 focuses on connecting governments and corporates with resources from the startup ecosystem. Through this work, he has interfaced with dozens of Fortune 500 corporates interested in leveraging engagements with entrepreneurs and emerging technologies towards innovative outcomes.

At the age of 23, Zafer co-founded his first company: Modern Media, LTD, a Jordanian media house that owns and operates two popular radio stations, including the award winning radio station Play 99.6, which received recognition from the US-based National Association for Broadcasters in 2007. Zafer was also a co-founder of The Online Project, where his team developed and executed digital strategies for Fortune 500 companies and high profile organizations operating in the Middle East and North Africa. In 2014 Modern Media was acquired by D&C Electronics, one of the region’s leading media conglomerates.


James Riney

Head, 500 Startups Japan

James Riney is the Head of 500 Startups’ Japan fund, and was recently listed on Forbes’ 30 Under 30 Asia list. Prior to 500 Startups, James covered early stage international investments for DeNA‘s venture arm, focusing on Silicon Valley and select countries in Asia. He drove DeNA’s first investments in Thailand, Indonesia, Philippines, and India. His investments have raised from Andreessen Horowitz, Khosla Impact (Vinod Khosla), Innovation Endeavors (Eric Schmidt), Global Founders Capital (Rocket Internet), and Biz Stone (co-founder of Twitter).

Before that, he was the founding CEO of STORYS.JP, the “Medium.com of Japan”. He was previously a banker at J.P. Morgan in New York and Tokyo.


Sriram Prakash

Global Lead of M&A Insight, Deloitte

Sriram Prakash is the Global Lead of M&A Insight. Based in London, he leads a high-profile multidisciplinary team that seeks to position Deloitte as a leader for market eminence and works with clients to drive insight-led deal opportunities. He has been quoted in publications such as The Economist, NY Times, WSJ and his research on corporate cash received front page coverage in The Financial Times. He is a regular commentator on M&A trends on Bloomberg, CNBC and BBC.

He is also one of the innovation leaders at Deloitte, focused on growth capital and corporate venturing.


Brian Gorbett

US Startups, Microsoft

Brian Gorbett has been with Microsoft over 9 years responsible for engaging technology startups, accelerators, and all those that foster the growth of startups. As a former founder, he mentors entrepreneurs building on the latest technologies and advises tech startups on the latest software architecture and business development opportunities.

Gorbett currently cares for Microsoft’s startup initiative for the West Coast of the US and actively seeks out passionate technology startups with innovative ideas. An active developer and hacker, he has contributed to national publications on software technologies and still tries to find time to get his own ideas into code. Prior to leading the West Coast startup initiative, Gorbett was responsible for investments in North, Central, and South America with Microsoft Ventures.

Before joining Microsoft, Gorbett built out Google’s startup program and managed a team of developers and designers responsible for delivering next generation apps.
